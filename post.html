Lets Talk About What When Wrong With Espisodes VII-IX

Problems look mighty small from 150 miles up

Posted by Generally Greaving on November 24, 2022

Most Star Wars fans can agree that so far the Disney produced movies have left much to be desired. Star Wars Espisodes 7 & 9 really went out of their way to provide fans with what they thought they wanted. With plots that very closely mirrored that of the of the original trilogy ( with Espisode 8 being the execption).

The brought back old characters in the Han solo, Luke, Lea, and ofcouse Chewie as well as many other fixtures of the fanchise. They also went out of their way in introducing a wide range of new characters, casting a diverse group of not well know but talented actors. Which brought great energy to the films and created much contriversy and anticipation.

When it was announced that Disney had puchased Lucas Films, with a with an offer that would rival many countries annual budgets. People came from far and wide, podcasting, posting, blogging about how amzing the news was and their anticipation of the greatness that we may be witness to in our not to far futures! When Espisode VII's trailer came out it registers millions of rushed to youtube to be the first to see it. And it did not disapoint. The trailer featured awe shattering visuals, a score that drove you to chills and goosebumps, and left wanting so much more. It had the whole fandom in a frenzy for almost a year.

So what Went Wrong?

Well we will start off with Espisode VII, and probaly the best of the three. But it still left so much on the table, as an introduction to the rest of the story it was great. Though as we neared the end of the movie we saw that it was a complete rehash of A New Hope (Espisode IV). With then had many fans revistiting the most of the ploy and realized that there were no new ideas, or any realy new story introduced in the movie at al, just nice acting and pretty lights. This left an awful taste in with many star wars fans, but yet there was still hope, the movie overall was not at the end horrible.

Then Espisodes VIII and IX were released, and though fans still went out and payed for them they, and as they say "money talks", and history will look at them as a box office sucess, but in truth they were anything but in the minds of their fans. In Espisode VIII we were treated to many classic holywood tropes, from love storie, to car chase scenes. All of which were did not move the story foward in anyway. The movie though original and had some promise at the end, as in itself a bad movie for it's content and made almost no sense at all.

Which now brings us to Espisode IX. In an attempt to save the trilogy, the producers tried to redcon the and provide explantions for the miscues of the previous movie. But in there attempt they ended up making the same mistakes of VIII, and just remade a movie from the original trilogy this time though in the form of Return of the Jedi. But unlike VIII fans were objectively upset and disapointed.

Where do we from here?

Well to be hosnest, there are not many options. Having ruined the main timeline of the Star wars universe, the only choice left for disney is to look at trying something from fresh in a whole new timeline, with new charcters and new stories.
